Who are we raising money for and why?

The Elena Baltacha Tennis Foundation
Elena Baltacha was a world class Scottish tennis player who died at the age of 30 of liver cancer. Not only was she an exceptional tennis player but she also left a legacy in the form of the Elena Baltacha Foundation which aims to make tennis accessible for children regardless of their background. The coaching team at Western have been inspired by "Bally" and are working with EBAT on a partnership to extend her legacy in Glasgow. We are absolutely delighted that Judy Murray will be attending our event to speak on behalf of this charity of which she is a patron. http://www.ebatuk.org/elena-baltacha-foundation/

Playlist for Life
We couldn't run this event without our dedicated team of Scottish celebrities! We consulted with the team and they helped us to nominate this charity that helps people with dementia to connect to their memories through music. We are delighted that Sally Magnusson will be attending our event to speak on behalf of this charity which she founded. http://www.playlistforlife.org.uk

The Scottish Dementia Clinical Research Network
This charity has been nominated by the family of well known Scottish tennis player, Malcolm Watt who has frontotemporal dementia. They wanted to make a donation to an organisation that is exploring causes for this condition. Malcolm has surpassed doctors' expectations by continuing to play at a high standard since diagnosis and he will take part in the event as one of our "pros". We are delighted that Pasna Sallis (Occupational Therapist, NHSGGC working with Malcolm Watt) will speak on behalf of SCDRN. http://www.sdcrn.org.uk

Click on this link to watch a video that shows Malcom playing tennis, plus, family and others speaking about occupational therapy intervention in regards to his diagnosis. https://letstalkaboutdementia.wordpress.com/2015/01/15/actualising-work-potential-in-dementia-care/
